
Consumer Protection Act – Rule Logic Design
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE
This file defines the rule-based legal reasoning logic used by the Consumer Law Decision-Support Chatbot.
The rules convert user-described problems into:
Issue type
Relevant legal provisions (indicative)
Risk level
Timeline range
Penalty awareness
Cost awareness
Escalation warnings
⚠️ These rules provide legal awareness only, not legal advice.
2. SUPPORTED LEGAL DOMAIN
Primary Domain: Consumer Protection Act, 2019
Scope: Consumer disputes related to goods, services, and e-commerce
Excluded: Criminal law, family law, labour law, verdict prediction
3. ISSUE CLASSIFICATION RULES
The system classifies the user problem into one primary issue type based on keywords and intent detected through NLP.
3.1 Supported Issue Types
Defective Product
Refund Not Given
Service Deficiency
E-Commerce Issue
Misleading Advertisement
General Consumer Query
3.2 Issue Identification Logic
Defective Product
Keywords: broken, damaged, defective, not working, faulty, malfunction
Refund Not Given
Keywords: refund, money back, return denied, no refund, seller refused
Service Deficiency
Keywords: delay, poor service, incomplete service, negligence, bad service
E-Commerce Issue
Keywords: online, app, website, delivery app, e-commerce, platform
Misleading Advertisement
Keywords: fake offer, misleading ad, false claim, advertisement lie
General Consumer Query
Used when no strong issue keywords are detected
4. SECTION MAPPING RULES (INDICATIVE)
The system maps issue types to commonly associated legal provisions under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.
Issue Type	Indicative Legal Provisions
Defective Product	Definition of defect, Section 35 (Filing of complaint)
Refund Not Given	Unfair trade practice provisions
Service Deficiency	Definition of deficiency, Section 35
E-Commerce Issue	Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020
Misleading Advertisement	Penalty for false or misleading advertisements
General Query	General provisions of the Act
⚠️ Sections are informational, not directives.
5. RISK SCORING RULES
Risk levels indicate legal exposure, not chances of winning.
5.1 Risk Levels
Low Risk: Informational or minor issue
Medium Risk: Monetary loss or service dispute
High Risk: Severe violations, penalties, or repeated misconduct
5.2 Risk Determination Logic
Condition	Risk Level
General legal awareness query	Low
Refund or service-related dispute	Medium
Repeated violation or high monetary loss	High
Penalty or imprisonment provisions involved	High
6. TIMELINE ESTIMATION RULES (INDICATIVE)
Timelines are approximate and depend on forum workload and case complexity.
Forum Type	Typical Timeline
District Consumer Commission	3–6 months
State Consumer Commission	6–12 months
Mediation Process	Shorter duration
Timeline is selected based on claim value and issue type.
7. PENALTY & CONSEQUENCE RULES
Penalties are displayed only if applicable.
7.1 Penalty Conditions
Misleading Advertisement
Fine may extend up to ₹10 lakh
Repeat offence may attract higher penalties
Non-compliance with Commission Orders
Fine and/or imprisonment as per the Act
Defective Goods / Refund Issues
Compensation may be awarded depending on loss
⚠️ Exact penalty amounts are never calculated.
8. COST AWARENESS RULES
Cost awareness is shown as broad brackets, not exact figures.
Case Type	Cost Awareness
Small consumer dispute	Low
Refund / service issue	Low to Moderate
Higher-value claim	Moderate
Costs may include:
Filing fees (often minimal)
Optional legal assistance
9. ESCALATION RULES (ETHICAL GUARDRAIL)
The system must recommend professional legal consultation if any of the following are detected:
9.1 Escalation Triggers
Criminal terms (fraud, cheating, police, FIR)
High penalty or imprisonment provisions
Repeated non-compliance by seller/service provider
User mentions arrest, summons, or prosecution
9.2 Escalation Output
If triggered:
“Consultation with a qualified legal professional is recommended.”
If not triggered:
“Immediate escalation may not be required.”
10. GENAI USAGE RULE (IMPORTANT)
GenAI (e.g., LLaMA) is used only for explanation
GenAI does not:
Select sections
Determine risk
Predict outcomes
Give legal advice
All legal decisions come from these rules.
